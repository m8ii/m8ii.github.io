Overview

In the heart of Brooklyn, New York, Ali cherishes his neighborhood. He lives with his 11-year-old sister, Jazz, and their strong-willed mother, Doris. Despite the ever-present challenges of drugs, gangs, and trouble, Ali manages to steer clear. Doris emphasizes the importance of education, viewing it as a gateway to opportunities. She works tirelessly at two jobs, determined to prevent her children from following their father's troubled path. Jazz is exceptionally bright for her age, and Ali takes immense pride in her. Their bond is strong, and Ali recognizes its value.

Characters

Allen (Ali)

A decent, churchgoing kid navigating the challenges of growing up in Brooklyn. Proud of his sister Jazz and values their bond. Ali makes some bad mistakes with significant consequences.

Jasmine (Jazz)

Ali's witty 11-year-old sister, known for her knack for giving nicknames and her intelligence. She shares a close bond with Ali.

Doris

Ali and Jazz's no-nonsense mom, a social worker working hard to raise her children right. Works two jobs to keep them from following their father's path.

Noodles (Roland)

Ali's best friend. Often shares meals at Ali's home. Known for his false bravado and occasional unwarranted aggression, which Ali dislikes.

Needles (Ricky)

Another close friend with Tourette's syndrome. With Doris's help, he manages his condition and is rarely seen without his knitting needles.

Plot Summary

The story unfolds in the Bedford-Stuyvesant section of Brooklyn, highlighting Ali, his sister Jazz, and their relationships with family and friends like Noodles and Needles. It explores themes of family, acceptance, and belonging within the context of black culture.

Next door to Ali is a notorious building. Five years earlier, two brothers moved in with their mother. Now about 15 years old, their home life contrasts sharply with Ali's.

When they get an invitation to a party, they seize the opportunity. Amidst alcohol, loud music, and older girls, Ali feels out of his depth. However, more significant events are about to unfold.

His boxing lessons help save a friend's life, but the aftermath terrifies Ali. He witnesses a different side of Noodles, followed by threats and violence. Ali wonders if life has changed forever for him and those he cares about.

Themes

  • Family: The story examines the complexities of family relationships.
  • Acceptance and Belonging: Characters seek acceptance despite cultural differences.
  • Black Culture: Offers insight into American, black culture.
